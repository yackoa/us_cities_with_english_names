Thoughts before further Analysis

The map of USA with state names looks like the following:

To proceed further we need to define which cities fall under east coast and west coast. One way is to use the latitude-longitude data for each city, but that would involve more analysis and data crunching to figure out in which coast each city lies. But if we go down that route we might need to consider the non-standard shape of the country which would make the analysis harder.

An easier approach is to use a more widely recognized definition from wikipedia which classifies as below:

  • East coast of USA implies the coastal states that have shoreline on the Atlantic Ocean
  • West coast of USA implies the coastal states that have shoreline on the Pacafic Ocean

Notes

  • Alaska & Hawaii was never colonized by the British. so I will not be considering them in the current analysis. The rationale is given here with data to support it. Please note that this is a debatable topic

  • Fun Fact : Alaska was purchased from Russia by the then 'United states'. Russia did not sell it to UK for reasons that UK doesn't get a stronghold there. But both UK & US was approached in hopes of a bidding war. But the then British Prime Minister Lord Palmerston steadfastly rejected the offer, arguing that Canada had enough uncharted wilderness to deal with and that Britain would overstretch its resources in maintaining Alaska. Hence Alaska was purchased by the US for a today equivalent of a little over 100 million dollars

  • Pennsylvania
    While Pennsylvania is not directly along the Atlantic shoreline, it borders the tidal portion of the Delaware River, and the city of Philadelphia was a major seaport. It was also part of the 13 colonies of UK. Hence we will consider it in our analysis Read more here

  • Fun Fact : The original thirteen colonies of Great Britain in North America all lay along the East Coast. see citation

Fun fact: According to the following infograph from this article_British) we can see that UK never colonized the lower part ie South western US. Check the cool GIF below which indicates the same

So for our analysis we can summarize the costal states as :

  • West cost states are : California, Oregon, Washington, and Alaska. reference
  • East coast states are : Maine, New Hampshire, Massachusetts, Rhode Island, Connecticut, New York, New Jersey, Delaware, Maryland, Virginia, North Carolina, South Carolina, Georgia, and Florida. reference

Inferences

Since the plot was created with US costal cities that have english names, we can derive few inferences from it.

  • There are certainly more cities in the East coast with English names than in the West coast
  • The density of the plotted points in the East coast is more than the West. Which means such cities are more close together in the East.
  • In the East coast we can easily see lots of English cities plotted along the shore line itself. This trend is lesser in the west coast.

All this further strengthens the orginal proposed hypothesis

3. Conlusion¶

  • We can see that when we calculated the percentage of the cities with english names in USA the West coast leads by marginal difference

  • Peronally I wouldn't bank on the difference since we haven't considered all the factors, like influence of other countries or similar sounding names. So we need more thorough tests to actually figure out if the difference is valid

So we can colude that the hypothesis stands, ie we have more number of cities with English names in East coast than the West

Limiations of the analysis¶

  • As mentioned earlier we are only considering a equivalent name matching scenario, we aren't considering similar sounding names or different spelling variations for city names. In the future may be I can try to use a distance matching algorithm (eg:eucledian) to find the closeness of the names.

  • Ideally need to use the longitude and latitude instead of relying on geographical method of segregating the states.

  • We havn't really accounted for year in which a city was named. For example Wales in Alaska was named in 1890s well after the UK left the US. There could be more such scenarios

Thoughts on including Hawaii & Alaska

Case in point Wales, AK, USA. Even though this Wales (US) was never colonized by the UK, its name is derived from the Wales in UK. This happend around 1890s. So there could be names in cities in Hawaii or Alaska that could have some very distant relation to the English names

While I drilled through the below data I found that there were only 23 such english names. Everyone of them were from Alaska. Regardless this wouldn't statiscally change the result by a huge factor (Yes I checked it), since total number of cities for AK & HI = 1145. Since they are in the West coast it affected only the percentage in the West coast alone, specifically lowered it to 5.13%. That brings the difference in percentages to 0.64%

Regardless it wasn't enough to refute the hypothesis. I know its debatable, but in reality we need to consider more things rather than direct name based equality comparisons right?
